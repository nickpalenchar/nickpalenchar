## The Main Objective

 > 
 > Enjoy a healthy lifestyle by eating real foods that don't raise cholesterol. Do this  with a **high fiber**, **high protein**, **low saturated fat** diet.

## Principles

### Opt for Whole Food, Vegan Meals

Cholesterol is found in most animal products and dairy. Processed grains (white bread, non-whole wheat) digest too quickly, increasing the likelihood of overeating.

## "Next Best" Compromises

The best diet is one you can maintain. I'm not 100% in my diet for various reasons (sometimes its not available, sometimes I need/want to cook more traditional meals for friends/events). This is my guide to what I should try to opt for, in rough order from best to worse.

* **Tortilla chips** - I break this rule once or twice weekly. It's a very small sacrifice (some brands are better than others)
* **White fish** - Depending on what i'm ordering there is more of a taste preference:
  * For "cakes" - mahi mahi, monkfish, halibut
  * otherwise: cod, haddock, tilapia
* **Salmon** - a bit higher in saturated fat than white fish, otherwise good. I also really enjoy salmon so it's a 1x/month treat
* **Bread** (0.5/week) in order of
  * Non-whole wheat
  * Rye
  * white wraps
  * white bread/buns (try for open-face)
* **Chicken** (1/month) I really enjoy cooking, and chicken is one of my favorite things to make a great meal out of for others. If I'm cooking chicken for others, I'll have some myself (often a smaller portion). I love connecting with others through food, which is difficult to do if you make an entirely separate meal.
* **Eggs** (1-2/quarter): too much cholesterol overall for my goals

---

* *(I pretty much never go below this line)*
* **Butter** - The only time I have this is when I can't control how my meal is made (such as at restaurants). In my own kitchen, there's no butter on anything, leaving me some wiggle room for eating out (but ordering veggie forward dishes.)
* **Red Meat** (1/half-year) - The only real exception is a steak dinner with friends, and even that is more for the experience. My own (hot?) take?: Chicken is far superior to steak. Incidentally, I also stopped eating burgers years before this entire diet (after getting food poisoning 🤮)
* **Sugar/baked goods** - Another shame as I really enjoy baking, but I try to keep this as low as possible. I also don't add sugar in my coffee or oatmeal (I use fruit and cinnamon instead). Sugar just causes too many problems.
* **Dairy/Milk** - I never have this, mostly because it had been very easy for me to cut it out. I've never used much milk/cream in my diet (and my cream based pastas always felt too heavy anyway)
* **Cheese** (\<1/year) - This specifically raises LDL Cholesterol.
* **Fried Food** (0) - Terrible.

## Alcohol

I still drink in moderation and enjoy the camaraderie it brings. I try to follow this order:

* None: Sometimes I can get away
* Red wine
* Gin & soda water
* Beer (1, light, and no other compromises that night). I do love beer and this is the thing I probably miss most.

## My Rotation

**Proteins** (on order of frequencies):

* **Beans** - Black, refried, cannellini, and kidney (in that order). I've grown to absolutely love beans, and find its the easiest and cheapest way to throw together a meal. When I'm short in time, I'll grill some veggies and throw it in a wrap with refried beans and wild rice. Top with taco hot sauce and I got a delicious and healthy meal in 20 minutes or less.
* **Tofu** - Some complain that tofu has no flavor, but to me its a blank canvas *for* flavor. Some of my favorite dishes:
  * Tofu and sweet Potato peanut curry - I find that the tofu "blends in" with the soft, warm sweet potato as its escort--it literally tricks the taste buds into thinking there's no tofu here.
  * [Chinese Braised Tofu](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RABvHkesIQc) by RainbowPlantLife
  * Looking forward to trying Buttery Gochujang Tofu and Broccoli.
  * If all else fails, just a simple [Stir fry](https://nickiscooking.com/recipes/the-stir-fry-blueprint/)
* **Lentils** (all kinds, usually red or green). Exploring a slowcooker spicy coconut-lentil soup. I'm still exploring the possibilities of this more.
